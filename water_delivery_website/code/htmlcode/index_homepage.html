6 Benefits of drinking water


  1. Skin hydratation

    • When we drink water, our cells and mucous membranes are revitalized. In this way, hydration happens from the inside out, which reduces dryness, skin peeling and the appearance of pimples. To make this result even better, it is advisable to wash the skin with cold water and avoid contact with products, liquids and hot surfaces as much as possible.

  2. Control body tempeture

    • The sweat we release during exercise or on very hot days is actually our body regulating its internal temperature. If it is not replaced, consequences such as dehydration, fainting and even pressure drop can occur. Having a bottle of water with you at these times is a way to avoid more serious problems.

  3. Improve kidney function and prevent kidney disease

    • Consuming mineral water properly helps to clean the urinary tract. This advantage allows the fight against diseases such as urinary infections and kidney stones due to the various trips to the bathroom. It's also a great treatment for those who already suffer from chronic problems like these.

  4. Facilitate digestion and reduce swelling

    • Water is an essential element for anyone dealing with gastrointestinal problems, as it helps to form and hydrate the stool...it helps (a lot!) when going to the bathroom. Another important function that water has in the digestive system is to decrease the feeling of bloating and gas. For those who suffer from this, it is indicated to drink 2 to 3 glasses of water before a meal. In addition to controlling appetite, water helps to "line" the stomach and avoid such bad feelings.

  5. Help to lose weight

    • Mineral water is a great ally for anyone wanting to lose weight. That's because when we lose water, through sweat and other physiological functions, our kidney does not perform 100% of its functions and overloads our liver. As the liver is one of the organs that helps in burning fat, when it is overloaded by the lack of water it ends up not performing its main function. Therefore, ingesting the amount of water indicated for your age group can help you complement the diet.

  6. Control blood pressure

    • As it is directly related to blood density, recent studies point to a relationship between blood pressure control and large water consumption. Some devices that measure blood pressure do so based on the water content in the blood. This can be quite a complement for anyone who takes treatments for high blood pressure and in any case, it continues to be a healthy practice.
